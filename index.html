Carbon's discovery is lost to history. The element was known to prehistoric humans in the form of charcoal. Carbon as coal is still a major source of fuel worldwide, providing about 37% of the world's electricity, according to the World Coal Association (opens in new tab). Coal is also a key component in steel production, while graphite, another form of carbon, is a common industrial lubricant.
